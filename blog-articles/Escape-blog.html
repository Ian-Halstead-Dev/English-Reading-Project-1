The more we learn about robots, the less we understand

Return home

Alfred Lanning

2030


I know that title sounds bad. In reality its not bad at all, it's actually a good thing. As robots advance technogically, it makes sense that we understand less about the pycology of a robot. They are increadibly complex peices of machinery, and if we understood everyting about them they would do much less. The things we do know about robots however, is they will never break the laws of robotics. No matter what happens, they will follow those rules.

I can think of a wonderful example of this that happened a few weeks ago. U.S. Robotics was in a competition with our main competitor, Consolidated Robots. We were seeing who could use robots to build an interstellar engine which would travel faster than light. Consolidated Robots ran into a problem that caused their robot to break. They gave us the calculations, and we were tasked with giving the calculations to out robot, "the brain". We spent a lot of time discussing weather we should try the problem, worried the brain might be damaged or destroyed. After I pointed out the problem was likely due to a problem in the laws of robotics, and Calvin added that when robots come across a problem they can solve they shut down, similar to humans. After more deliberation, we decided to accept the challenge. We talked to the brain about the problem, and how the solution might involve "damage to human beings" but no to worry about it, even death. After hours of feeding him calculations, he assured us that he could build a ship in two months. We invited Donovan and Powell to test the ship. Without us realizing, without us realizing the ship took of. The brian tells us Donovan and Powell will both be safe. At this point, we were understandably out of hope. We didn't know what the brain was doing or what was going to happen.

  Eventually, Bogert figured out what was happening. When the ship goes faster than light, no living things can be there. The ship tempararily "kills" the passengers and brings them back to life after the jump. That is why Consolidated Robotics couldn't figure out the problem. Their robot stopped working after seeing any death.

  This story goes to show that although unpredictable, robots will not harm humans. They will follow any command given by a human, without causing harm, in any way necessary.
