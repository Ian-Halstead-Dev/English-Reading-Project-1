Physical pain is not the only kind

Return home

Susan Calvin

2021


When I think of pain I immediately think of physical hurting, and I imagine many others would as well. That is what most people think robots are forbidden to do by the first law, hurting humans physically. We have learned today that that is not entirely true. Robots still will never be able to hurt humans physically, but we were forgetting people can be hurt mentally or emotionally, and robots will never hurt a human mentally. Herbie was created with the ability to read minds, but nobody knows why he has this ability. Alfred Lanning, Peter Bogert, Milton Ashe, and I (all officers for US Robotics) all decided to figure out what the problem with Herbie was and to not tell anyone else about the robots ability. I was directed to study the robot and try to figure out what may have given him the power, while Ashe goes to the assembly line to determine the problem there. While talking to the robot, I realized he must know about the love I feel towards Ashe. Of course, he does, but assures me that he never told Ashe anything. While I was talking to the robot, I started to get very upset. Ashe is younger, and is more attractive, and she doesn't think Ashe has feelings for her.. Herbie told me that wasn't true and that Ashe does have feelings for me, and I was shocked. I accuse him of lying, and think he is thinking of the wrong girl, but Herbie assured me he was talking about me. A day later however, I met with Ashe where he told me he was getting married to a different girl. Herbie had lied to me! I tried to keep my feelings hidden while I went outside to talk with Herbie. As I was talking to him, Bogert and Lanning both burst into the room claiming Herbie lied to them as well. Herbie just stood there, unable to explain what he had told anyone and suddenly something clicked in my head. Of course Herbie lied, he can't cause any pain to humans, including mental. Herbie continued to stand there, until he suddenly collapsed on the ground.

  I've written this to show how far robots will go to obey the first law. No robot will ever hurt you, or and other human. They are forbidden to, and will take juristic measures to make sure humans are never hurt.
